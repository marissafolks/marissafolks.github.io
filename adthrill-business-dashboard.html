UX Design · Data Visualization · Dashboard Redesign

A business analytics dashboard providing marketing teams with insightful data.

AdThrill's B2B dashboard captures the analytics that power marketing strategy. Designed for marketing teams, product directors, and researchers, it's a centralized platform for analyzing consumer data and optimizing market research and advertising efforts.

Project Scope
B2B dashboard redesign
Role
Lead UX/UI Designer
Topics Covered
Data visualization
Team
Business owner, engineering, marketing
The Ask

How can we allow users to more meaningfully interact with their data?

Problem

Data was there. Insight wasn't.

Users were only able to view their data at a high level — there was no way to drill down into the details that actually mattered. On top of that, the data simply wasn't organized in a way that was intuitive to navigate.

The Goal

Make it possible to view data at a high level, a low level, and anywhere in between — with more flexible filtering and sorting capabilities along the way.

Deliverables

Shipped as incremental, individually usable milestones.

To keep the dashboard's existing functionality unhindered while changes rolled out, the redesign was broken into three deployable stages.

01

Interface UI upgrade

02

Ability to drill down data to its smallest form

03

Added sort/filter functionality

Process

Empathize, ideate, deliver.

01

User Feedback

Direct user feedback had already been collected by the business owner before kickoff, so that became the baseline for the solution — there was no budget at this stage for additional research.

  • Dashboard UI felt outdated
  • No ability to filter or sort data
  • No way to drill down into the data
02

Understanding the Data

Before redesigning anything, the underlying data itself needed to be understood. The dashboard compared ad campaign performance, positive vs. negative votes, votes by rating, and votes by date — each graph had to present that data in a way users could read at a glance.

03

Sketches

Before opening Figma, I sketched the layout of the new dashboard for its priority pages and brought those sketches to the business owner and lead engineer to kick off a discussion on expectations and technical feasibility.

04

High-Fidelity Wireframes

Once the direction was approved, the updated style guide was incorporated into high-fidelity designs for the business dashboard.

Final Design

Data visualization and customization.

Filtering and sorting functionality was added to the votes list, giving users a much more flexible way to access and manipulate the data that mattered to them — including filtering a graph by date, filtering a paginated table by gender and rating, and sorting a paginated table by date.

Final iteration — high-fidelity dashboard wireframes
Summary

These enhancements enabled users to interact more effectively with their data — letting them customize their view and analyze information from multiple perspectives.
